# About Steve

Steve is an anxious young adult gifted with the health and stamina of a middle-aged man.

He works as a front-end developer and ill-equipped designer at an insurance company.

He recently purchased a punching bag.

He was told by his hair stylist that—while he can rest assured his hair is not thinning—it is receding and has sprouted some grays. Even though he had been suspect of this phenomenon years prior, it didn’t hurt any less to hear the news.

He enjoys playing video games, although his middle-aged complacency has affected this aspect of his life as well. He misses the days when all you were expected to do in a shooting game was shoot—now, [one is expected to build a fortress][1] while doing so.

While general existence in the modern world has blessed him with residual anxiety, nothing quiet does it like holding babies, answering the phone, grocery shopping, or paperwork. In fact, he almost didn’t go to college because of the amount of paperwork. Fortunately, he had a responsible sister that filled out the application and—if his memory serves—paid the application fee as well. In more recent times, his lovely wife has had the pleasure of dealing with his aversion to tedious administration. In fact, he has been so titillated with her work, that he briefly looked into giving her guardianship over him...but that was even more paperwork.

He has a lot of bad ideas—and is still paying for all of their domains. Writing that last quip reminds him he may still have a subscription to Naturebox that is still going to his old dorm room.

He has spent countless hours attempting to make his home _smart_, only to find himself yelling “ALEXA TURN OFF THE BEDROOM LIGHT!” many, many times every night.

One time, he shit in a public restroom with the stall door wide open. This, of course, was unintentional—the grave error was only realized once he looked up, locking eyes—through the mirror—with a gentleman washing his hands. He is quite certain that man did not sing happy birthday to himself twice over before rinsing the soap off.

Currently, his favorite ECMAScript 2015 addition is the [spread operator][2]: `{ ...props }`.

Lately, his Instagram feed has consisted of a variety of whey proteins and people, just like, stretching.

He spends about \$50 a week at Starbucks and thinks their coffee is ‘okay’. His favorite beverage is the _Flat White Blonde_, although he admits he may not have remember the name without error.

He makes charitable donations to animal shelters and political campaigns—expecting nothing in return—only to get badgered with mail that MUST cost more than his donations (In a brief proofreading, to keep him honest, his wife suggested he clarify: _she_ makes the donations to the animal shelters, in his name).

He wouldn’t consider himself a hipster, but is fond of his [wool shoes][3]—you can decide.

He buys a lot of fancy devices, justifying they will help him with his ‘work’. He then proceeds to spend weeks setting up the perfect _workflow_ only to realize the old thing worked better for him anyway.

His wife once introduced him to her coworkers, most lackadaisical, with “eh, he is something to do.”

He recently found an [amazing budgeting app](https://ynab.com)—he realizes that might be a contributing factor to his wife’s lukewarm feelings about him.

Like most everyone else, he does his best thinking in the shower. This, clearly, was not thought up in the shower.

# Get in Touch

Please, if I’ve said anything dimwitted (likely)--or you just want to chat--get in touch with me. I’ve purposely decided against having post comments, but that doesn't mean I don’t want to hear from you! Feedback helps me get better at writing pieces you enjoy reading.

If you like my work--software, design, writing or otherwise--drop me a line. While I have a day job, I won’t turn down work that I find interesting!

Finally, if you find any issues with the site please do let me know, especially if they are accessibility problems—web standards are important to me and I believe in providing the best experience possible for all users.
