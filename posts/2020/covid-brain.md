This is as good of time as any to remind everyone that Covid-19 [is still very much alive](https://www.nytimes.com/2020/07/08/world/coronavirus-updates.html?action=click&module=Top%20Stories&pgtype=Homepage#link-775463ee) and even more dangerous than we originally thought. Melinda Wenner Moyer, in a New York Times op-ed:

> Among patients hospitalized for Covid-19 in Wuhan, China, more than [a third](https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jamaneurology/fullarticle/2764549) experienced nervous system symptoms, including seizures and impaired consciousness. Earlier this month, French researchers [reported](https://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMc2008597) that 84 percent of Covid patients who had been admitted to the I.C.U. experienced neurological problems, and that 33 percent continued to act confused and disoriented when they were discharged.

Any bit of rules I’ve been lax on are back to being applied strictly now. It’s easy to let up, but we all need to be vigilant for each other. Wear masks. Keep your distance. Don’t gather indoors if you can help it.
