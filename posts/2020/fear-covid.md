It's hard to look at any reputable news site right now and not [see how bad the United States' has mismanaged the coronavirus][1].

The fact is that we are currently in the depths of the crisis and still not taking it very seriously. My timelines are filled with bachelorette parties, boat outings, and crowded beaches—hell, even the neighbors down the street threw a party with 20 people crowded on their porch. 

It's easy to not see the effects of the virus and assume it is overblown. Even with it raging out of control, there is a good chance that you don't know of anyone that has been diagnosed with it. In this time of government failure, it is important that we all still try our best to be responsible about stopping the spread of the virus. In the times I start to think that I could ease up a little on my restrictions, I'm met with [another article describing the misfortunes of living months with symptoms from the virus][3] and quickly batten down the hatches. 

If current partisan fighting over masks is any indication of how a vaccine will go, we are in for [a long road to normalcy][2]. While we don't have a vaccine we should all be encouraging each other to stay home if possible, as well as social distance and wear a mask when necessarily in public.
