Epic Games blatantly broke the AppStore rules by including their own purchase processing and probably deserve to be removed from the store in this instance.

If I had to guess, I imagine they knew Fortnite would be removed from the store and are adding fuel to the fire started by [Hey](https://hey.com/apple/) and continued by [xCloud](https://www.theverge.com/2020/8/5/21356274/microsoft-xcloud-ios-apple-iphone-ipad-testing-ends-apple-app-store-policies). This happening all around the time [Tim Cook testified that the AppStore isn't a powerful gatekeeper and all developers are treated equal](https://thehill.com/policy/technology/509506-read-apple-ceo-tim-cook-testimony-before-house-panel).

As a developer currently working on an iOS only app, I cannot say I'm comforted by the way Apple has been treating third-party developers. The Hey controversy was frustrating and showed that Apple sees developers as a nuisance and not a value add. In my case, I'm consciously using Apple services—like CloudKit—I might otherwise not use to help justify the 30% I'll be handing over to Apple.

As a consumer I'm downright disappointed. xCloud looks awesome—being able to play Halo on an iPad seems like magic and would have been unimaginable just a few years ago. Microsoft invested deeply to make it possible and now petty politics is getting in the way.

Here is to hoping Apple will change it's policies (forcedly by policy, or otherwise) because it is hurting everyone involved.
