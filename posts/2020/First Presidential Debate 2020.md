What a mess. 

I understand that the country is *polarized*. 

I get that I have my own biases. In theory I'd like to consider myself an independent—a free thinker—but in practice I'm effectively a Democrat. Though, this presidential election *feels* different than the last three I've participated in (granted, I felt similar in 2016, but a little more naive and hopeful). 

The country is *polarized*. 

I'm just not sure how we got here. Some consequential portion of the nation is okay with white supremacy? They are okay with misogyny? They don't see the importance of a free press? They don't value honesty? They are fine with a president meddling in an election?

These aren't controversial new ideas. These are values we have collectively agreed upon over time—some since the beginning of our time. They are gimmes. I'm not sure how one can see Trump as anything other than a miserable fraud only looking out for himself—at the cost of our democracy.

The country is *polarized*. 

...but if you watched the same damn debate I saw yesterday—the one where [Trump called on the Proud Boys to stand by](https://www.washingtonpost.com/nation/2020/09/30/proudboys1001/) and [refused to concede the election in the case of a loss](https://www.nytimes.com/2020/09/30/us/politics/trump-debate-election.html) then *polarized* we will remain—because, fundamentally, you are a piece of shit.
