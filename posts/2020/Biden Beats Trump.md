I've waited a day to post on this because I'm unsure how to feel and approach the matter. 

On one hand, I'm incredibly happy that our country showed up [in record numbers](https://www.washingtonpost.com/graphics/2020/elections/voter-turnout/) to repudiate the scum that is Trump and his enablers. Hell, I lost it last night when [Biden mentioned he would be gathering a team of experts to implement a course of action against COVID-19](https://buildbackbetter.com/priorities/covid-19/)—something that seems so sensible in light of the current administration's strategy of wishing it away.

On the other hand, it's concerning to me that as it stands currently, Trump amassed over 71 million votes. I struggle to understand how anyone could vote for him. Voting on partisan issues I get, but you need a functioning democracy to continue to have partisan issues—Trump openly challenged our democracy, not hiding it in the least bit. All other issues are sidelined when democracy is threatened—even the egregious issues, like equal justice. If you do not believe Trump threatened democracy, I cannot help you. He attacks the free press and he has attempted to seed distrust in our election. He has effectively said: "I can do no wrong, negative reporting is false. I am so popular that if I lose the election it was stolen". Yet people flock to him. They scream "stop the count" for absentee ballots that have always been counted after election day. They wave Trump flags, worshipping their dictator. Not all Trump supporters are these extremists—they are instead enablers. The Christians without a moral compass. The "both sides do bad things" group. There is no equivalence in wrong doing to be made between the Republicans and Democrats in recent history. You are living in an alternative reality if you truly see this as a sports ball match between your home team and their rival.

If someone as [dumb as Donald Trump](https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/video/2017/aug/21/donald-trump-look-directly-sun-eclipse-video) can become President of the United States of America there are serious retrospectives to be had. We narrowly escaped, but the American Experiment continues. Let's celebrate for now, but Christ do we have some soul searching to do.
