title: What if I Don't Want to Play?
---

Growing up you are told that you have to do things that you do not want to do. Regardless of the reasoning your guardians gave you—cliche being to learn responsibilities—I was always aware that the reasoning was unfounded. Mowing the lawn doesn’t make me more responsible, it makes your grass shorter for a much cheaper rate than you would find any professional charging (to be fair, the job was worse as well, I guess my heart wasn’t in it).

While it felt unfair, it also seemed temporary, eventually I would get older, be financially stable, and prioritize my life in such a way that I would have fewer ‘responsibilities’—which really are just chores of modern life. As an adult we have a choice of how we handle our responsibilities, at least to a certain extent. I didn’t come from money, but it cannot be ignored that I’m very privileged to be in a position to trade responsibilities for money at this point in my life (which I totally recommend if you have the means to do). The problem comes in when you don’t have options to trade money for certain responsibilities.
